# WGU Brand and Design System FAQ

Common questions, with definitive answers.

## Brand basics

**Q. What is WGU's mission?**
A. To change lives for the better by creating pathways to opportunity.

**Q. What is the brand's voice?**
A. Empowering, Authentic, Inviting, Modern, Bold, Professional.

**Q. What pronoun do we use when speaking to students?**
A. "You". The student is the protagonist. WGU is the enabler.

**Q. Can I use emoji in WGU marketing copy?**
A. No. Emoji are not used in WGU brand collateral. Use the icon system instead.

**Q. Can I make an owl pun?**
A. No. No owl puns, no owl-themed wordplay, ever.

## Color

**Q. What is the exact WGU Blue?**
A. #002855.

**Q. Does WGU Blue have to appear on every asset?**
A. Yes. It is the unifying color and is required on every designed asset.

**Q. Can I lighten WGU Blue for a hover state?**
A. No. Never tint, lighten, darken, or eyeball brand colors. Use the exact HEX from the palette. For interactive states, use Medium Blue (#0070F0) and step to deeper blues only with the approved hover values.

**Q. Can I use Lime Green for a button?**
A. No. Lime is an accent for one "pop" moment per layout. Use Medium Blue for primary buttons.

**Q. What gradient should I use for a hero CTA?**
A. The Medium Blue gradient: `linear-gradient(135deg, #002855 0%, #0070F0 100%)`.

## Typography

**Q. What is the primary typeface?**
A. Futura PT. Arial is the approved system fallback.

**Q. We do not have Futura PT licensed for our project. What do we do?**
A. For production, license Futura PT or use Arial as fallback. The design system uses Jost as a free Google Fonts substitute for prototyping. Flag the substitution in production work.

**Q. When can I use Rocky?**
A. Only for isolated infographic numerals. Not for body copy. Not for headlines.

**Q. When can I use Program Nar OT Black?**
A. Only for Education for the People campaign headlines.

**Q. Is Inter or Helvetica acceptable?**
A. No. Use Futura PT (or Arial fallback) only.

## Logos and marks

**Q. Can I combine the wordmark and the owl icon into a custom logo?**
A. No. They are two separate marks and are used independently.

**Q. Can I use the owl icon on a public marketing page?**
A. The owl icon alone is reserved for student, alumni, and employee audiences. Use the university logo for public-facing comms.

**Q. What is the minimum logo size?**
A. 52px wide on web. 0.725 inch wide in print.

**Q. Where do I download official logo files?**
A. The WGU DAM (Canto). University logos: wgu.canto.com/b/JJP30.

**Q. Can I put the Academic Seal on a t-shirt?**
A. No. Apparel uses must be cleared with Brand Governance.

## Iconography

**Q. What icon library should I use in product UI?**
A. The official Simple Icons family for navigation. The design system substitutes Lucide where the official set is unavailable.

**Q. Can I use Illustrated icons in product UI?**
A. No. Illustrated icons are for marketing collateral.

**Q. Can I create a new icon for my project?**
A. No. Only the Brand Governance Team adds to the icon library. Email brand.wgu.edu if your need is not covered.

## Photography

**Q. Can I use stock photography on a WGU page?**
A. Avoid. Use authentic WGU student photography from Canto. Stock should only be a last resort.

**Q. What is the approved photo style?**
A. Cinematic, vibrant, warm-toned. Real students in real environments. Minimal retouching. Authentic grit.

**Q. Can I tint a photo blue?**
A. Only with approved gradient overlays for headline legibility (the protection gradient or the Midnight gradient over photography). Do not invent custom color washes.

## Patterns

**Q. Can I use two patterns in one layout?**
A. No. One pattern per asset.

**Q. Can I recolor a pattern?**
A. No. Use the supplied colorways (navy, blue, light tint).

**Q. Can I lay copy directly on a pattern?**
A. Only with explicit Creative Team approval. Otherwise place copy on a content panel above the pattern.

## Components

**Q. How tall should a default button be?**
A. 44px.

**Q. Can I disable the focus ring?**
A. No. The focus ring is always visible on interactive elements.

**Q. What is the default card radius?**
A. 8px (`--radius-md`). 16px (`--radius-lg`) for feature cards. 999px (`--radius-pill`) for tags and CTA pills.

**Q. Should I use validation on every keystroke?**
A. No. Validate on blur. It is less punitive.

## Motion

**Q. Are bouncy animations okay?**
A. No. WGU motion is restrained, ease-out only. No bounces, no springs, no elastic.

**Q. How long should a hover transition be?**
A. 120ms.

**Q. How do I handle reduced motion preferences?**
A. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`. Reduce transition and animation durations to near-zero.

## Education for the People campaign

**Q. What is the campaign's POV?**
A. "Saying the quiet part out loud. College has become inaccessible for many. WGU has always stood with students."

**Q. What fonts are unique to the campaign?**
A. Program Nar OT Black for headlines. Futura PT Book for body.

**Q. What is the campaign palette?**
A. Deep Blue (#003057), Sky Blue (#46B1EF), Lime Green (#97E152), White.

**Q. Is half-tone treatment acceptable outside the campaign?**
A. No. Half-tone portraits are campaign-specific.

## Process and governance

**Q. Where do I send a brand approval request?**
A. brand.wgu.edu.

**Q. Does the Claude plugin replace Brand Governance review?**
A. No. The plugin is for prototyping and education. Public-facing campaigns, co-branded assets, and Academic Seal use still require Brand Governance review.

**Q. Where can I see an interactive brand sandbox?**
A. The `wgu-design` Claude plugin includes preview cards, slide archetypes, and a marketing-site UI kit. Contact Bentley Folkman for the plugin.

**Q. Where is the official brand guide?**
A. https://www.brand.wgu.edu/.

## Accessibility

**Q. What accessibility standard does WGU target?**
A. WCAG 2.1 AA at minimum.

**Q. Brand and accessibility conflict on my project. Which wins?**
A. Accessibility. Choose a compliant pairing from the safe list. Brand correctness does not override accessibility.

**Q. Is the focus ring optional?**
A. No.
