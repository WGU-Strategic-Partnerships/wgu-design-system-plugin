# WGU Photography and Imagery

## Visual character

WGU photography is **cinematic, vibrant, warm in skin tone, and authentically gritty**. It does not look stocky, retouched-to-death, or "stage-managed". The visual language reflects real students at real moments in their lives.

## Approved photographic treatments

### 1. Standard documentary

Real students in their environments. Warm, vibrant color. Minimal retouching. Natural light wherever possible. Shot to feel observational rather than posed.

### 2. Student cutouts

Silhouetted students placed on solid brand-color backgrounds (typically WGU Blue or Midnight). The cutout treatment is used in marketing layouts where the student needs to be the focal point and the background needs to read as a brand surface.

### 3. Half-tone treated portraits (Education for the People campaign)

Half-tone-style printed portraits on brand-color backgrounds. Used only for the Education for the People campaign (Dec 2025–present). Not a default treatment.

## Photography rules

### Do

- Use authentic student imagery whenever possible.
- Keep skin tones warm and accurate. Diversity of representation is a brand priority.
- Allow grain, texture, and the imperfection of real environments.
- Shoot wide enough to give the subject context (workspace, family, classroom).

### Don't

- Don't over-retouch. WGU students are real people; smoothing them out reads dishonest.
- Don't use generic stock photography that does not feel like a WGU student.
- Don't crop in extremely close on faces unless the campaign calls for it (e.g. half-tone portraits).
- Don't tint photos with color washes outside the approved gradient overlays.

## Gradient overlays on photos

Photography in WGU layouts often pairs with one of two gradient overlays to keep typography legible.

- **Blue gradient overlay paired with bold white headlines.** Used for hero photos with text on top. Apply as a top-to-bottom or bottom-to-top fade.
- **White gradient overlay paired with Blue Midnight or Midnight text.** Used when the layout calls for a lighter feel and the photo is dark.

The protection gradient (`linear-gradient(180deg, rgba(0,23,49,0) 0%, rgba(0,23,49,0.85) 100%)`) is a function, not a decoration. It exists so the headline reads cleanly. Do not use protection gradients on a photo that does not have text on it.

## Photo composition guidance

- The student is the focal point. The campus, environment, or activity is supporting context.
- Eye-level or slightly low-angle perspective conveys agency and dignity.
- Avoid awkward shallow depth of field that obscures context.
- Frame so there is "breathing room" for typography on at least one side or the bottom.

## Use of the academic and campus environment

WGU is fully online. Avoid photography that implies a traditional brick-and-mortar campus experience (lecture halls full of seated students, ivy-covered buildings, dorm rooms full of underclassmen). Show students learning in their actual contexts: at home, at work, in the community.

## Where to find approved photography

Approved imagery lives in the WGU DAM (Canto). Contact brand@groups.wgu.edu for access. Do not source WGU imagery from web search results, and do not repurpose imagery from third-party publishers.

## Imagery and accessibility

- Always provide alt text. Describe the subject and the action, not just "student".
- Avoid imagery that conveys important information through visual cues alone (an arrow drawn in the photo, a chart embedded in the photo). Provide the same information in text.
- Be mindful of color contrast where text overlays photography.
