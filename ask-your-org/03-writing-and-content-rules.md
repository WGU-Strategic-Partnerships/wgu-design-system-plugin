# WGU Writing and Content Rules

These rules apply to every piece of WGU copy, in every channel.

## Less is best

Simple, direct language. Get to the point. The brand guide caps copy length by format:

| Format | Max copy |
| --- | --- |
| Out-of-home billboard | 7 words |
| 15-second video | 25 words |
| Paid social headline | 27 characters |
| Email subject line | ~50 characters |
| Push notification | 1 short sentence |

If copy runs long, cut it. Do not shrink the type to fit.

## Audience-first framing

Before writing any piece, answer three questions:

1. Who will read this?
2. What do they need to know?
3. What action should they take?

If you cannot answer all three, the copy is not ready.

## Conversational, never too casual

Professional and respectful, but approachable. Not stiff. Not slangy. The bar is "thoughtful colleague at a serious organization", not "friend at brunch" and not "boilerplate corporate".

## Active voice and strong verbs

- "Advance your career." Not "Your career can be advanced."
- "Earn your degree." Not "A degree may be earned."
- "Own your future." Not "Have ownership over your future."

## Evidence-backed claims

Every claim must be sourceable. Per the brand guide, "Unverified quotes and stats can't be included." Prefer specific numbers and named outcomes.

- Good: "75+ industry-aligned programs."
- Good: "On average, WGU graduates see a salary increase within two years."
- Bad: "World-class education."
- Bad: "The best online university."

## Casing rules

- **Sentence case** for most UI labels and body headlines.
- **Title Case** is acceptable for marquee marketing headlines and section eyebrows.
- **ALL CAPS** is reserved for short eyebrow labels, some infographic titles, and Education for the People campaign headlines.

Avoid Title Case in product UI. It feels dated and institutional.

## Numbers

Use digits for statistics and data. Examples: "75+ programs", "100% online", "1:1 mentorship". In infographics, isolated numerals are set in **Rocky** serif (substitute Newsreader in this design system) for visual elevation.

## Punctuation and typography

- Use the Oxford comma.
- Use straight or smart quotes consistently within a single asset.
- Use en dashes for ranges (Mon–Fri, 9–5).
- Use em dashes sparingly. The WGU voice prefers shorter sentences over heavy interruption.
- Avoid exclamation points outside genuine celebratory moments.

## Owl rules

- The Sage mascot costume and the official owl logo are the only approved owl appearances.
- No owl puns. No owl-themed wordplay. No "hoot", no "wise owl", no "talons", no "perch", no "nest", no "fly". Even internally in headers, document titles, or Slack channel names targeted at brand audiences.

## Emoji

No emoji in marketing or brand collateral. WGU is a professional brand. Use the icon system instead. Emoji may appear in ad-hoc internal social contexts but are not part of the design system.

## Examples in the WGU voice

**Hero or marketing headline**

> Own the next chapter of your career. Earn an accredited, competency-based degree online, on your schedule, at a flat tuition rate.

**Student portal greeting**

> Welcome back, Jamie. You've completed 4 of 6 competencies in this term. Keep going. Your next assessment is ready when you are.

**Stat or infographic line**

> 75+ industry-aligned programs. 100% online. 1:1 mentorship from day one.

**CTA microcopy**

> Apply now. Request info. Transfer your credits. Talk to an enrollment counselor.

**What to avoid**

> "Hoot hoot. Our wise owls will guide your journey." Breaks the no-owl-puns rule and the no-emoji rule, and reads as too casual for a serious institution.
