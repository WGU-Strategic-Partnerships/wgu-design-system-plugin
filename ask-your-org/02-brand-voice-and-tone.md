# WGU Brand Voice and Tone

## The six voice characteristics

WGU's voice is **Empowering, Authentic, Inviting, Modern, Bold, Professional**. Every piece of WGU communication, from a billboard to a button label, should feel like it could only have come from WGU.

- **Empowering.** The student is the protagonist. WGU is the enabler. Copy puts the student in motion ("Earn your degree", "Own your future").
- **Authentic.** Real students, real outcomes, real numbers. No platitudes, no corporate hedging, no manufactured emotion.
- **Inviting.** The brand opens the door rather than closing it. Plain language, no jargon, never gatekeeping.
- **Modern.** Forward-looking, clean, current. The brand never reads dated or institutional in the worst sense.
- **Bold.** Direct claims, strong verbs, definitive statements. Confidence without arrogance.
- **Professional.** Always credible, never sloppy. Even at its most conversational, WGU sounds like a serious university.

## Tone shifts by context

The voice (who WGU is) is constant. The tone (how WGU sounds) flexes by context.

| Context | Tone leans toward |
| --- | --- |
| Acquisition and marketing | Bolder, aspirational, headline-led |
| Student portal and learning UI | Calmer, supportive, second-person, instructional |
| Operational and policy comms | More precise, more formal, evidence-led |
| Employer and B2B | Outcomes-focused, data-led, partnership framing |
| Alumni | Celebratory, reflective, community-led |
| Crisis or sensitive comms | Calm, transparent, accountable, direct |

## Pronouns

- **"You"** for the student. Used constantly in headlines, CTAs, and product UI. The student is the hero.
- **"We"** for WGU as an institution. Used sparingly and intentionally ("We believe...", "We help..."). Avoid stacking "we" sentences.
- **"They"** for any third party (employers, faculty), used neutrally.

Never refer to students as "they" in headlines aimed at students. Always speak directly to them.

## Voice do and don't

**Do**

- Lead with the student outcome.
- Use active voice and strong verbs ("Advance", "Earn", "Own").
- Use specific numbers and named outcomes whenever possible.
- Keep sentences short. Aim for clarity over cleverness.

**Don't**

- Use owl puns or owl-related wordplay. Even internally.
- Use emoji in marketing or brand collateral.
- Hedge ("may help", "could potentially"). State the claim or cut it.
- Use hype or buzzwords ("revolutionary", "best in class").
- Refer to WGU students in the third person when speaking to them.
