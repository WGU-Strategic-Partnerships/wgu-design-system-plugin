# WGU Patterns and Backgrounds

## The eight brand patterns

WGU has a system of eight brand patterns. They are decorative textures intended to add depth and brand personality to surfaces without overwhelming content.

| Pattern | Feel | Common use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Swirl** | Curving organic motion | Hero backdrops, section dividers |
| **Network** | Grid of interlinked nodes | Tech, partnership, employer pages |
| **Comms** | Speech-bubble shapes | Communication, messaging surfaces |
| **Connect** | Linked dots and lines | Community, networking |
| **Flow** | Wave forms | Calmer, supportive sections |
| **Elevate** | Upward chevrons or arrows | Outcomes, achievement |
| **Growth** | Branching plant motif | Personal growth, journeys |
| **Pathways** | Layered roads or routes | Programs, learning paths |

Each pattern is available in three colorways: **navy**, **blue**, and **light tint**.

## Pattern rules

### Do

- Use one pattern per asset. The brand reads as confused when two patterns share a layout.
- Use patterns at low contrast as a backdrop for content that has its own focal point.
- Match the pattern's tone to the content (Pathways for program pages, Network for partnerships, etc.).

### Don't

- Don't place two different patterns on the same asset.
- Don't modify patterns. No recoloring outside the supplied colorways. No rotating, no skewing, no scaling that introduces banding.
- Don't layer copy directly on a pattern without explicit Creative Team approval. Patterns are background surfaces; copy goes on solid panels above them.
- Don't use patterns as content. They are texture, not message.

## Backgrounds

Most WGU layouts work from a small set of background surfaces.

### Solid

- **White (#FFFFFF).** Default content surface.
- **Light Blue (#EEF6F9).** Tinted sectional surface, used to separate adjacent sections without strong borders.
- **Light Grey (#F1F1F1).** Neutral fill for chips, secondary cards, and inactive states.
- **WGU Blue (#002855).** Most heavy-duty hero, footer, and dark section surface.
- **Midnight (#001731).** Deepest dark surface, footers, gradient base.

### Gradients (web only)

- **Midnight gradient (`#001731 to #002855`).** Top-to-bottom for full-bleed dark sections.
- **Medium Blue gradient (`#002855 to #0070F0`).** Diagonal (135deg) for "hot" CTA moments, quote blocks, apply-now banners.

### Pattern surfaces

Place a pattern on a solid surface (typically WGU Blue or Light Blue) and overlay content panels with adequate contrast.

## Layering hierarchy

When stacking surfaces, work outward from the content.

1. **Type and content** sit on a clean panel.
2. **Panel** is a solid surface (white, light blue, or WGU Blue) with appropriate radius and shadow.
3. **Pattern** sits beneath the panel as decorative texture.
4. **Background** is solid color or gradient.

| Section | Pattern |
| --- | --- |

## Where the official pattern files live

Official pattern files live in the WGU DAM (Canto). The WGU Design System ships PNG copies of all eight patterns in their three colorways under `assets/patterns/` for prototyping. For production, pull the high-resolution sources from Canto.

## Transparency and overlay

Transparency is used **only on protection gradients over photography** to keep headlines legible. It is not used as a decorative treatment on brand colors themselves. The brand guide forbids tinting and opacity on the palette.
